For an unprejudiced reader, more eager to understand than to judge, to reason than to conjecture, reading this book by Professor Vittorio Mathieu of the University of Turin will produce contradictory feelings. Perhaps it is more helpful to begin with the negative ones. The reader interested in the objectivity of reasoning will be overcome with unease while savoring this Philosophy of Money, whose title revives Simmel's reflection. How can one not feel disturbed when one realizes that there are people who write clearly, who thoroughly analyze the issues they address, who rigorously present the results of their observations on economic behavior, yet whose work is barely acknowledged? Just as there is a propensity for liquidity, isn't there also an irresistible propensity to refuse to understand the clearer the explanation? If this is a general tendency, it becomes especially acute when discussing economic matters, and even more so when someone spells out the word "money."
